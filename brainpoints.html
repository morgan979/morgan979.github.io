BrainPoints

Developing a text editor to increase retention in introductory programming courses

Skills

Agile Research
Usability Studies
Prototyping

Problem and Opportunity

Students drop out of introductory programming courses at rates of 30- 50%
  • Programming editors and IDEs place people in a fixed mindset which commonly leads people to believe they are inherently bad at computer science
  • Promoting the growth mindset in Computer Science may increase retention especially among women and minorities

Our Work

Conducted needfinding, ran user studies, and developed a code editor
  • Researched and identified that people with a growth mindset can attribute their process to their outcome
  • Designed an online code editor that allows us to give participants popups at appropriate times that would help them identify their process
  • Ran studies with students from introductory programming classes to measure fluctuation in their growth mindset and desire to persist through challenges

Results and Future Work

High potential of a text editor that correctly detects process and appropriately rewards good strategies
  • Future iterations of this text editor that automatically detects good behavior may increase retention in introductory computer science classes, especially among women and minorities
