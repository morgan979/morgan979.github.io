BrainPoints

Developing a text editor to increase retention in introductory programming courses

Skills

Agile Research
Usability Studies
Prototyping

Problem and Opportunity

Students drop out of introductory programming courses at rates of 30- 50%
  • Programming editors and IDEs inherently place people in a fixed mindset
  • Promoting the growth mindset in Computer Science may increase retention

Our Work

Conducted needfinding, ran user studies, and developed a code editor
  • Researched and identified that people with a growth mindset can attribute their process to their outcome
  • Designed an online code editor that allows us to give participants popups at appropriate times that would help them attribute their process to their outcome
  • Ran studies with students from introductory programming classes to measure fluctuation in their growth mindset and desire to persist through challenge

Results and Future Work

Positive participant feedback
  • High potential of a text editor that correctly detects process and appropriately rewards good process
  • Future iterations of this project may increase retention and a text editor that automatically detects good behavior shows great potential
